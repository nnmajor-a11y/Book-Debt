Frequently Asked Questions:

"Are Book Debts only for tracking how many chapter books you read and buy?"
Not at all! A Book Debt is designed to get the most out of all the books a bookworm buys and owns, including comic books and manga. A general rule of thumb when it comes to graphic novels and manga is this:
One comic or manga volume of a series or collection is a fraction of that series's entirety.
For example, let's say you bought a two-part comic series. Each comic is valued at 0.5 debt & credit, unlike a chapter book's full credit & debt. This is to encourage the graphic novel readers to appreciate their comics through multiple reads. After all, reading a comic book is usually faster and less demanding. Following this rule encourages Book Debt users to reread their comics and appreciate the detailed artworks poured into each panel!
"I finished the 100 rows provided. Do I have to download another Book Debt Excel sheet and start another one?"
If you would like to download another Book Debt, you are always welcome to download as many Book Debt sheets from the download page.
However, if you want to continue dedication to a single Book Debt, yes, there is a way to add more functional rows in your current sheet through the copy and paste tools in Google Sheets or the Microsoft Excel program.

How to Add Rows in the Book Debt Sheet :
  1. Ensure there are at least two blank rows available at the end of the Book Debt sheet (with alternating background colors).
  2. Highlight the row containing the Total Read and Total Bought boxes — from the Books columns (Title, Author) across to the Debt column (Evaluation Integer).
  3. Drag the selection down to cover the number of new rows you want to add.
  4. With the rows still highlighted, go to the Insert tab, select Rows, and click Insert [x] Rows Above.
    The number of highlighted rows will now become blank rows with empty Evaluation Integer cells, inserted just above the Total Read and Total Bought row.
  5. Next, select the two available and pre-formatted rows from the Book Debt template.
  6. You'll see a small dot at the bottom-right corner of the selection.
  7. Click and drag the dot down to fill over the newly inserted rows.
    This will copy the formatting and functions — including the alternating background color pattern — into the new rows.
"Do I have to use this sheet to determine if I can buy more books? What if I have kids whom I want to encourage to read, but want to reward them with something other than other than more books?"
The Evaluation of your Next Purchase cell can remain an unidentified credit and debit system. Even if your goal for earning credits isn't for buying books, the system will still calculate how many books you have read or must read before buying your next purchase on any item.
You can edit the Book Debt's Can I Buy a New Book? cell to say: "Can I Buy a New Video Game?"; "Can I Buy a New Outfit?"; "Can I Buy a New Toy?" etc. So long as you read the number of books recorded in your Book Debt.
"If I already have a book, and I read it, would I still have to put a tally in the Bought? "
In short, no. In long, if you wish to consider a book you already own as part of your debt, then you can.
For example, if you have owned books you wish to read first before buying anymore, record these books in your Book Debt.
If you own books that weren't obtained through purchase, or you owned books before starting a Book Debt, but you don't want to count it as debt, then you would list the book without tallying the Bought column. Doing it this way can help bring down your accumulated debt and encourage you to read any books you already have.
Both options are valid for encouraging you to read your previously owned books. Considering your already-obtained books as part of your debt are also optional to make you read the most out of them before buying new books.
"Is there a Way to customize and change the Colors of my Book Debt?"
Yes. Once you've downloaded the Book Debt from the Download page, it's yours to edit and stylize. However, keep the format structure. This includes: The title bar, the headings, and functions in the Evaluation of your next purchase cell & Evaluation Integer column.
